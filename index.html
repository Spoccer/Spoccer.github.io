Simulation of COVID-19 spread in schools.

This is an attempt to show with a mathematical model the effects that social distancing and mask wearing would have on the spread of COVID-19 within a school. A detailed methodology of the model is shown at the bottom of the page. The sliders below allow you to change some of the variables into the model.

This chart shows the simulated cases over an entire school year.

Remember that mathematical models are typically WRONG, but they can be USEFUL.
Simulations have random variation programmed into them, so you should run more than one simulation at each setting to get a feel for the variety of outcomes that are possible.
